## Glaze 1: "Honey Shino" — Cone 6 Oxidation
**Style:** Textured/crazed cream-to-yellow shino
**Surface:** Satin to semi-gloss with orange-peel texture
**Crazing:** Intentional (CTE 81.4)
**Food Safe:** ✅ (clean chemistry, iron only colorant)

| Material | % |
|---|---|

### UMF
- SiO2: 2.55 | Al2O3: 0.58
- Na2O: 0.31 | K2O: 0.10 | CaO: 0.30 | MgO: 0.13 | SrO: 0.17

### Notes
- Apply thick for more texture, thin for subtle craze
- Try 1-3% iron oxide for cream → yellow → amber range
- Na2O and Al2O3 slightly over standard limits — intentional for shino character
- Strontium gives warmer tone than pure calcium bases
- Specific gravity: start at ~1.45, adjust for dipping

## Glaze 2: "Copper Dust" — Blue-Green Tea Dust
**Style:** Crystalline tea dust with blue-green color shift
**Surface:** Glossy with crystal formation on surface
**Food Safe:** ⚠️ Test for copper leaching — better as exterior glaze
**Experimental:** Iron+copper crystal interaction — results will vary with cooling schedule

### Base Recipe (100%)
| Material | % |
|---|---|
| Nepheline Syenite | 38.0 |
| Silica | 22.0 |
| Whiting | 15.0 |
| Ferro Frit 3134 | 10.0 |
| Dolomite | 5.0 |
| Talc | 5.0 |
| EPK Kaolin | 5.0 |

### Additions
| Material | % |
|---|---|
| Red Iron Oxide | 6.0 |
| Rutile | 4.0 |
| Copper Carbonate | 2.5 |
| Cobalt Carbonate | 0.3 |
| Bentonite | 2.0 |

### UMF
- SiO2: 2.41 | Al2O3: 0.28
- Na2O: 0.20 | K2O: 0.05 | CaO: 0.57 | MgO: 0.18 | B2O3: 0.09

### Notes
- SiO2 slightly low (2.41 vs 2.5 limit) — intentional for crystal fluidity
- 6% iron = crystal nucleation sites (the "dust")
- Copper+cobalt shift crystal color from brown/gold → blue-green/teal
- Rutile creates float patterns between crystal clusters
- SLOW COOL for more crystal development (hold 1050°C for 30-60 min)
- Try on Oregon Brown for contrast, NZ 6 for cleaner color read

### Application Notes
1. Dip or pour Oribe base over entire piece
2. Let dry completely
3. Spray or lightly dip foam overlay on upper half / rim area
4. Where overlay runs into base during firing = foam/lava interaction zone
5. SiC in overlay creates gas bubbles → foam texture
6. High CTE overlay crawls apart → exposes green base through white foam gaps
7. Vary overlay coverage for different effects

## Test Tile Plan
For each glaze, make at least 3 test tiles:
- **Thin application** (1 dip / light spray)
- **Medium application** (2 dips / standard)
- **Thick application** (3 dips / heavy)

Test on both **NZ 6 porcelain** and **Oregon Brown stoneware**.

For Glaze 3 (layered), also vary:
- Overlay coverage: 1/4, 1/2, 3/4 of tile
- Overlay thickness: light spray vs heavy spray

Fire to **Cone 6** with standard schedule.
For Glaze 2 (Copper Dust), also try a **slow cool** hold at 1050°C for 30-60 min.
